Satellite Rendezvous and Docking in LEO

This project was for Professor Howell's Modeling and Simulation at Purdue University. Our task was to model the rendezvous of two spacecraft in orbit and create a video of them docking in orbit. Otherwise, the project was open-ended, and we applied a linear error-feedback controller to the spacecrafts' attitudes so they could dock properly.

The semester concluded with a presentation to NASA Johnson Space Center . Presentation (PDF), Report (PDF), and Video (AVI)

Vicarious Calibration of GOSAT Satellite

More information:

Presentation (PDF)

Paper (PDF)

The goal of this research was to provide validation for GOSAT radiance values measured at three different wavelengths (760nm, 1600nm, 2060nm). I performed field operations support and the necessary data analysis to achieve this goal.

There were two components to this work:

1. Measurement of the surface radiance at Railroad Valley, NV (chosen for its homogeneity in reflectance).

2. Analysis of spectrometer data and generation of scale factor used for calibration of GOSAT data.

The reflectance values of another satellite, MODIS, were compared to the data collected by GOSAT. Whereas GOSAT takes a single measurement of a large footprint, MODIS takes an image with pixels about the same site as the areas measured on the ground (500x500m). The ratio of a single MODIS pixel (corresponding to Automated Spectrometer ASD site) to the average of select MODIS pixels (corresponding to the GOSAT footprint) was used to generate a scale factor between the GOSAT measurement and the ground measurement. That is, the degradation of the MODIS pixels was assumed to be uniform across the image, so the ratio of the reflectance at one ASD site to the entire GOSAT footprint would be the same no matter how much the MODIS sensors had degraded. This factor would serve as a conversion between the GOSAT measurements with degradation to the calbrated GOSAT measurements.

Scale Factor Code

Defines GOSAT footprint as an ellipse using data points from JAXA team

Extracts pixels from MODIS image within GOSAT ellipse

Calculates ratios of MODIS ellipse average radiance to MODIS ASD radiance for each band

These ratios are necessary for calculating the overall surface reflectance of the area in the GOSAT footprint.

Inputs ratios to radiative transfer code

Small UAV Automatic Ground Collision Avoidance System

A carryover from a previous Air Force project on the F-16, the ultimate goal of this project is to install a Trajectory Prediction Algorithm with terrain mapping on General Aviation aircraft. I tested this algorithm and built the hardware-in-the-loop simulation to implement the software in a Piccolo Autopilot with a FlightGear interface. Later, I the ground operations for the small UAV that we used to characterize the terrain avoidance maneuvers.

Reconstruction of Pad Abort 1 Simulation with Flight Data

I reconstructed the ANTARES simulation of the Orion Pad Abort 1. I replaced the pre-flight models with the day-of-flight models (atmosphere, winds, engine temperature, updated mass properties, etc.) and ran Monte Carlo dispersions to test whether or not the trajectory matched the simulation within three-sigma uncertainty.
